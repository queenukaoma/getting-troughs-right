Nurse Quick Guide

Transplant C0 Trough Timing Protocol

Step 1 — Identify
❤️
Is your patient a heart transplant recipient?
🚪
Place the TRANSPLANT door sign
Step 2 — Timing
07:30
Check: is today's trough order present? If NOT, page/contact provider
08:30
Draw tacrolimus / sirolimus / cyclosporine trough
(window: 08:15 – 08:45)
09:00
Administer the scheduled dose immediately after the draw
🚫
Do NOT hold the dose to wait for the lab result.
Step 3 — Document
📝
Last-dose time = MAR "Given" time of the previous dose
⏰
Chart the actual clock time for each administration (not the scheduled time)
Step 4 — Escalate When:
🌙
Night shift drew with 03:00–04:00 morning labs (incorrect timing)
⚠️
The 08:30 window is missed (it is ≥09:00 and no trough was drawn)
❓
Any ambiguity about dosing/med times

Why This Matters

Tacrolimus and sirolimus have narrow therapeutic windows. A level drawn at the wrong time can look too high or too low. Wrong levels lead to unnecessary dose changes that get reversed in <72 hours. Correct timing = reliable levels = safer dosing decisions.
